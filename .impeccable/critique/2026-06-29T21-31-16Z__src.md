## Anti-Patterns Verdict

### LLM Assessment
The visual composition feels extremely premium and authoritative, perfectly fitting the BUMN and banking target demographics. The interface stays completely clear of generic AI-cream/beige templates and follows the structured geometry of 12px-16px borders without over-rounding. Visual interest is driven by semantic elements (the audit maturity bar, active stats strip) rather than sketchy/doodle graphics or text-gradient slop.

### Deterministic Scan
No new issues detected outside of documented design system exceptions. All reported findings are covered by the ignore list:
- Font family selections (Inter & Space Grotesk) are confirmed brand design system constants.
- Custom scrollbar styles and border-radius specifications (4px scrollbar thumb) are documented exceptions.
- Glass container borders (`rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)`) are confirmed tokens in `DESIGN.md`.

## Overall Impression
Daya Solusi Integra's interface is state-of-the-art for its target market. It balances extreme corporate authority with premium modern dark interface aesthetics. The single biggest opportunity is hardening input validation on the contact form and refining focus ring highlights to ensure accessible keyboard-only navigation.

## What's Working
1. **The 10% Accent Rule:** The BUMN Gold accent is perfectly restrained, highlighting critical details (readiness levels, select badges, icons) without overwhelming the canvas.
2. **Interactive GRC Self-Assessment:** The maturity assessment wizard is exceptionally clean, responsive, and guides the user to a value-first AI report before transitioning to the contact stage.

## Priority Issues
- **[P1] Form validation feedback (Error Prevention)**: The phone number and email fields in [Contact.tsx](file:///C:/backup/Daya-Solusi-Integra/src/components/Contact.tsx) use simple HTML5 validations. Implementing custom real-time validations prevents submission of incomplete inputs.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable harden src/components/Contact.tsx`
- **[P2] Keyboard-focus indicator visibility (Accessibility)**: Hover/active focus rings on buttons and radio segments in [Assessment.tsx](file:///C:/backup/Daya-Solusi-Integra/src/components/Assessment.tsx) are hard to distinguish on dark navy containers.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable audit src`
- **[P2] Transition orchestrations (Aesthetic / Motion)**: Tab-switching inside the self-assessment and AI Consultant panels feel slightly abrupt. Soft transitions will premium-ize the interactive flow.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable animate src`

## Persona Red Flags

- **Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Keyboard-only navigation focus outlines in the Assessment options and segment buttons (`#sector-segment-control`) are faint, making it difficult to track focus placement against dark container panels.
- **Alex (Power User)**: While `Ctrl + /` shortcut works perfectly to open the AI advisor, option selections within the assessment wizard require cursor navigation or sequential tabs with no speed accelerators.
- **Riley (Deliberate Stress Tester)**: Inputting special symbols or oversized text into the company name setup screen does not trigger inline constraints, potentially sending messy inputs to the AI generation request.

## Minor Observations
- Custom scrollbar thumb (`#1e293b`) has excellent contrast but could transition to a brighter tone when active.
- Logo graphic in [Header.tsx](file:///C:/backup/Daya-Solusi-Integra/src/components/Header.tsx) is purely typographic; a solid geometric emblem could enhance the institutional look.

## Questions to Consider
- Should we prompt the user to auto-populate the Contact form with email/name if they've interacted with other sections?
- What would a custom offline error modal look like in the Assessment if the `/api/assess` endpoint fails?
