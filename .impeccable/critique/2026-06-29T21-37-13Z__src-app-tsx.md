## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Verdict: Borderline AI-Generated Feel**

*   **LLM Assessment (AI Slop & Visuals)**: The landing page presents a premium brand canvas. However, it falls into a few prominent template-like patterns:
    1.  **Section Eyebrows (Kicker Clichés)**: Standard tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows (`Layanan & Solusi`, `Fokus Sektor`, etc.) are placed above headings in every section. This directly violates the absolute ban on these eyebrows in `DESIGN.md`.
    2.  **Simulated Dashboard Layouts**: The Hero's glass panel mockup visualizes generic metrics (`98.4% Readiness`, `WTP Opinion`) that look clean but feel like typical AI-generated landing page filler rather than realistic project artifacts.
*   **Deterministic Scan Findings**: The automated detector discovered 5 issues in `src/index.css`:
    *   **Overused font (`overused-font`)**: `Inter` is flagged as an overused font that diminishes visual distinctiveness.
    *   **Colors outside `DESIGN.md` (`design-system-color`)**: Undocumented color values `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)` and `#1e293b` are hardcoded in CSS.
    *   **Radius outside `DESIGN.md` (`design-system-radius`)**: A hardcoded `border-radius: 4px` was detected.
*   **Visual Overlays**: Browser visualization was skipped due to local CLI constraints (Puppeteer not installed in workspace packages). No active UI overlays are rendered.

## Overall Impression
The interface has a powerful, authoritative look using BUMN Blue and Gold on an Ink Navy canvas. The interaction bridge between the assessment completion and the consultation form pre-fills is exceptionally well-engineered. However, the presence of banned section kicker eyebrows and undocumented CSS values slightly cheapens the custom feel of the site.

## What's Working
1.  **Maturity Data Bridge**: Automatically pre-filling the consult form with the assessment's metadata is a brilliant conversion optimization strategy.
2.  **Print-to-Board Styles**: Media queries optimized for printing turn the dark screen layout into clean, board-ready white compliance summaries.
3.  **Keyboard Efficiency**: Excellent accessibility support with keyboard shortcuts (hotkeys 1-4 for questions, Ctrl+/ for the AI drawer).

## Priority Issues

*   **[P1] Section Eyebrow Kicker Badges**
    *   **Why it matters**: Directly violates the project's visual rules and adds unnecessary visual noise that screams template.
    *   **Fix**: Remove the tiny uppercase tracked badges above headings or replace them with standard, untracked bold text.
    *   **Suggested command**: `$impeccable typeset`
*   **[P2] Inflexible Assessment Wizard Flow**
    *   **Why it matters**: A strict 10-question sequence creates high friction. Busy executives may abandon the flow before completing it.
    *   **Fix**: Add an option to skip or allow free-form click navigation between questions.
    *   **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout`
*   **[P3] Missing Inline Glossary / Definitions**
    *   **Why it matters**: Complex GRC acronyms (ITGC, Continuous Control Monitoring) lack tooltips, causing potential confusion for less technical management leads.
    *   **Fix**: Implement clean hover tooltips or abbreviations for regulatory terminology.
    *   **Suggested command**: `$impeccable clarify`

## Persona Red Flags

*   **Jordan (Risk Director - Time-Poor)**: Jordan wants a quick assessment of maturity. The mandatory 10-question linear sequence forces multiple clicks and prevents quick scanning. Jordan abandons at question 3.
*   **Casey (IT Security Analyst - Skeptical)**: Casey notices the simulated dashboard showing generic placeholders and questions the validity of the "AI Executive Report." Casey might view it as a marketing gimmick rather than professional consulting.

## Minor Observations
*   Hardcoded colors (`#1e293b` and `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)`) and border-radiuses (`4px`) in `src/index.css` bypass the tokens defined in `DESIGN.md`.
*   Floating AI trigger badge is highly visible but adds to screen clutter when multiple actions compete.

## Questions to Consider
1.  "Does our self-assessment build professional authority by requiring effort, or are we just creating a bottleneck for executives who want quick results?"
2.  "Why does our design documentation explicitly ban section kicker eyebrows while our codebase includes them in every single component?"
