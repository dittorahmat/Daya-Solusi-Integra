## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:**

2. **Glassmorphism Overuse:** Using `backdrop-filter: blur` as the default card container styling across the entire landing page instead of a purposeful layout choice.
3. **Left-Stripe Accent:** Using a 2px left border on the maturity indicator box instead of uniform geometric shapes.

## Overall Impression
Daya Solusi Integra provides an exceptionally high-fidelity, professional, and compliant dark interface that aligns perfectly with BUMN guidelines. However, several minor interactive anti-patterns (such as distracting floating trigger animations, ghost-card shadows, and repetitive glass cards) slightly compromise this aesthetic premium.

## What's Working
- **High-Fidelity Compliance Vocabulary:** The use of domain-specific terminology matches executive expectations perfectly.
- **Smart Conversational Flow:** Preserving the assessment's state to prefill the contact form drastically reduces user friction.
- **Strong Interactive Feedback:** Nav highlighting, keyboard shortcut guidance, and clean wizard step transition styling.

## Priority Issues
- **[P1] Ghost-Card Anti-Pattern**: `.glass-panel-hover:hover` pairs a 1px border with a 30px blur shadow (`box-shadow: 0 10px 30px -10px rgba(0,0,0,0.7)`). Similarly, the `#assessment-wizard` uses Tailwind's `shadow-2xl` (50px blur) alongside a 1px border.
  - *Why it matters:* Violates the "Flat-By-Rest" rule and the absolute ban on pairing 1px borders with soft shadows >16px blur. It undermines corporate authority, making cards look floaty and template-like.
  - *Fix:* Remove the large drop shadows. Keep the layout flat and utilize subtle, solid borders, or keep shadows under 8px blur.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable polish`
- **[P1] Excessive Glassmorphism Defaults**: Almost every card container, callout, and wizard background uses `.glass-panel` with a 12px background blur.
  - *Why it matters:* Violates the absolute ban: "Glassmorphism as default. Blurs and glass cards used decoratively. Rare and purposeful, or nothing." Layering multiple glass containers over ambient gradients creates visual mud.
  - *Fix:* Transition services and sector cards to flat, solid navy backgrounds (e.g., `#0d1e3d` or `bg-slate-900`) and reserve glass blurs for the navigation bar and the AI Advisor drawer.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable quieter`
- **[P2] Visual Side-Stripe Accent Border**: The maturity level container uses `border-l-2 border-bumn-blue` as a left accent stripe.
  - *Why it matters:* Violates the absolute ban: "Side-stripe borders. border-left or border-right greater than 1px as a colored accent on cards, list items, callouts, or alerts."
  - *Fix:* Change the left accent border to a uniform 1px border around the entire container (`border border-slate-800`).
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable layout`
- **[P2] Distracting Pinging Notification Dot**: The floating AI trigger button has an active `animate-ping` dot on the icon.
  - *Why it matters:* Creates constant movement in the viewport, distracting users from filling out form fields and violating the "Single focus" criteria.
  - *Fix:* Remove the `animate-ping` element. Use a static golden indicator.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable quieter`
- **[P2] Mobile Thumb Zone Obstruction & Reset Risk**: The floating AI badge is fixed in the bottom-right corner on mobile, which is a key thumb zone. Additionally, the wizard's state resets if the user leaves or reloads the tab.
  - *Why it matters:* High risk of accidental drawer launches. If the user reloads, their 10-step progress is lost.
  - *Fix:* Hide the floating badge on mobile (relying on the menu drawer CTA), and save assessment progress to `sessionStorage`.
  - *Suggested command:* `$impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags
- **Alex (Power User):** Alex wants to breeze through the wizard but is forced to wait for the 280ms auto-advance delay on each question click. The contact form requires typing out 7 fields with no option for quick business card uploads or rapid keyboard shortcuts.
- **Jordan (First-Timer):** Jordan might get confused when their Gmail address is rejected on the contact form, thinking the form is broken rather than understanding the corporate email policy. The floating trigger dot's constant pinging creates false urgency that may make Jordan feel anxious.
- **Bambang (BUMN Risk/Compliance Officer):** Bambang prints the report for the board, but any minor table overflow or text clipping in the print view will make him look unprofessional.

## Minor Observations
- The custom scrollbar style uses `#0b0f19` which matches the body background, making it hard to see the scroll track.
- The glossary tooltip lacks keyboard focus instruction, although it supports `tabIndex={0}`.
